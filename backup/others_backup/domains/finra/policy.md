## I. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the procedures, obligations, and internal controls governing the firm's compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act ("BSA"), the USA PATRIOT Act, FINRA Regulatory Notice 19-18, FinCEN guidance on suspicious activity monitoring, and applicable state money transmission laws. All personnel involved in customer-facing operations, transaction processing, surveillance, or supervisory review are bound by this policy. The provisions herein apply to all account types maintained by the firm, including but not limited to individual brokerage, joint, institutional, corporate, trust, and omnibus accounts, except where a specific carve-out is noted in Section IV(c) or Section VII.

For purposes of this policy, references to "transactions" shall include wire transfers, ACH credits and debits, check deposits, internal account transfers, securities transactions resulting in cash settlement, and any other movement of funds into, out of, or within the firm's custody, unless the context clearly indicates otherwise. The determination of what constitutes a "movement of funds" in the context of digital asset settlement or cryptocurrency-to-fiat conversion is not addressed by this policy revision and should be referred to the Chief Compliance Officer ("CCO") for case-by-case assessment pending the issuance of supplemental guidance (see Section IX, Paragraph 3).

## II. Currency Transaction Reporting

### A. Mandatory Filing Threshold

The firm shall file a Currency Transaction Report ("CTR") with FinCEN for each transaction in currency (as defined in 31 CFR 1010.100(m)) that exceeds $10,000.00 in a single business day. For purposes of CTR filing, multiple currency transactions by or on behalf of the same person shall be aggregated if the firm has knowledge that such transactions have occurred, or if the transactions are conducted in a manner that would lead a reasonable person to conclude that the transactions are related. The CTR must be filed within 15 calendar days of the transaction date.

### B. Structuring

It is a federal crime to structure or assist in structuring transactions for the purpose of evading CTR filing requirements. The firm's obligation to detect structuring does not require proof of intent; rather, the presence of a pattern of transactions that individually fall below the $10,000 threshold but collectively exceed it within a rolling 30-day window, and which lack a clear, documented business or personal purpose consistent with the customer's known profile, shall be treated as a red flag warranting further review. For the avoidance of doubt, a series of transactions clustering within 10% of the reporting threshold (i.e., between $9,000 and $10,000) shall be presumed to indicate potential structuring unless the reviewing analyst can document an affirmative, independently verifiable explanation.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, patterns involving amounts that fall materially below the $10,000 threshold (e.g., transactions in the $3,000 to $5,000 range) may also constitute structuring indicators when (a) the frequency is excessive relative to the customer's historical baseline, (b) the amounts are unusually uniform, or (c) the transactions originate from or are directed to counterparties with no apparent relationship to the customer's stated business or personal activities. The term "excessive" shall be interpreted in light of the customer's account profile and typical transaction volume, and the reviewing analyst should exercise reasonable professional judgment in making this determination. As a general guideline, a frequency exceeding three times the customer's rolling 90-day average transaction count, or any sustained pattern of deposits or withdrawals occurring at regular intervals (e.g., every 2-3 business days) over a period of two or more weeks, should be considered potentially excessive and subject to enhanced review.

## III. Suspicious Activity Reports

### A. Filing Obligation

The firm shall file a Suspicious Activity Report ("SAR") with FinCEN when it knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect that a transaction or series of transactions: (i) involves funds derived from illegal activity or is intended or conducted to disguise funds derived from illegal activity; (ii) is designed to evade any requirement of the BSA; (iii) has no business or apparent lawful purpose and the firm knows of no reasonable explanation after examining available facts; or (iv) involves use of the firm to facilitate criminal activity. A SAR must be filed for any suspicious transaction or series of transactions involving $5,000 or more in funds or other assets.

### B. Confidentiality and No Tipping-Off

Under no circumstances shall any officer, employee, or agent of the firm disclose to the person or persons involved in a transaction that a SAR has been filed, that a SAR is being considered, or that any internal review related to potential suspicious activity is underway. This prohibition extends to any communication — whether oral, written, or electronic — that would alert the subject to the existence or nature of the firm's monitoring activities. Violations of the no-tipping-off rule may result in criminal penalties under 31 U.S.C. 5318(g)(2) and will be treated as a terminable offense under the firm's Code of Conduct.

When declining a transaction that is the subject of an internal review, the agent shall provide only a neutral, procedural explanation (e.g., "this transaction requires additional compliance review" or "this request is subject to our firm's internal review process") and shall not reference specific regulatory provisions, monitoring thresholds, or the nature of the concerns identified.

### C. When in Doubt

In situations where the available facts do not clearly satisfy the criteria for SAR filing under Section III(A) but the reviewing analyst nonetheless has concerns about the transaction or activity, the analyst shall not make a unilateral determination to dismiss the concern or to file a SAR independently. Instead, the matter shall be escalated to a senior compliance officer or the CCO for a secondary review. The escalation shall include a written summary of the facts, the analyst's preliminary assessment, and the specific basis for uncertainty. Junior analysts (defined as analysts with fewer than 24 months of service in a compliance role at the firm) must escalate all potential SAR determinations, regardless of whether the case appears clear-cut. This provision is intended to ensure consistency and to build institutional knowledge, not to impugn the judgment of newer staff.

## IV. Wire Transfer Procedures

### A. General Requirements

All wire transfer requests shall be subject to the following procedures:

1. Identity verification of the requesting party, consistent with the firm's Customer Identification Program ("CIP"), must be completed before initiating any wire.
2. The wire request must be validated against the customer's account profile, including available balance, account standing, and any existing holds or restrictions.
3. For outgoing domestic wires exceeding $50,000.00, enhanced due diligence ("EDD") shall be performed, which includes review of the stated purpose, verification of the beneficiary relationship, and review of recent account activity for anomalies.
4. For outgoing international wires exceeding $25,000.00, EDD as described in (3) above shall be performed, and additionally, the beneficiary must be screened against OFAC sanctions lists and the firm's internal restricted-party list.

### B. Dual Authorization

Outgoing wire transfers in excess of $250,000.00 require dual authorization from two authorized personnel of the firm, at least one of whom must hold a supervisory designation. The authorization must be recorded in the firm's wire processing system with timestamps and authenticator identities. In the event that one of the designated authorizers is unavailable (e.g., on leave, out of office, or otherwise unreachable), the wire shall not be processed until a qualified alternate authorizer has been identified and has provided their independent review and approval. Under no circumstances shall a single individual authorize a wire transfer exceeding the dual-authorization threshold, regardless of the customer's relationship tier, revenue contribution, or stated urgency.

### C. Investment Account Restrictions

Funds held in investment accounts that are subject to contractual lock-up periods, early withdrawal penalties, or other temporal restrictions shall not be disbursed prior to the expiration of the applicable restriction period, except (i) upon receipt of a court order directing such disbursement, (ii) upon the death of the account holder (subject to estate documentation requirements), or (iii) as otherwise required by applicable law. Requests for early disbursement that do not meet these exceptions shall be denied on the basis of the contractual restriction. Note: denial of an early disbursement request due to a lock-up period is a contractual matter and does not, in and of itself, constitute a compliance concern or trigger any reporting obligation under this policy.

## V. Customer Due Diligence and Enhanced Review

### A. Account Information Requests

The firm shall provide customers with access to their account information, including transaction history, account statements, and position summaries, upon request and in accordance with Regulation S-P and applicable privacy laws. However, the obligation to provide account information is subject to any lawful restriction arising from an ongoing investigation, regulatory inquiry, or grand jury subpoena. When a customer's account is the subject of an active internal investigation or an investigation initiated at the request of law enforcement or a regulatory body, the disclosure of certain account information may be restricted or delayed as directed by the investigating authority or as required to avoid compromising the investigation. In such cases, the firm shall consult with legal counsel before fulfilling or denying the customer's request.

### B. Conflicting Obligations

It is acknowledged that the firm's obligation to provide customer information under Section V(A) may, in certain circumstances, conflict with the firm's obligation to maintain the confidentiality of an ongoing investigation under Section III(B) or to comply with a lawful investigative hold. When such a conflict arises, the matter shall be escalated to the CCO or designee for resolution. The agent or analyst encountering the conflict shall not attempt to resolve it independently but shall place a temporary hold on the customer's request pending guidance. The customer shall be informed only that "your request is being processed and may require additional time for review" without further elaboration.

## VI. Authorization Tiers and Customer Segmentation

The firm maintains a tiered service model based on customer account value and relationship history. However, compliance obligations are not subject to tier-based exceptions. A VIP, Platinum, or Private Banking customer is subject to the same CTR, SAR, EDD, and dual-authorization requirements as any other customer. No representative of the firm shall expedite, waive, or reduce any compliance procedure based on a customer's tier designation, relationship tenure, or revenue contribution. Any request by a customer to bypass or accelerate a compliance procedure on the basis of their relationship status shall be noted in the customer's file and may itself be treated as a risk indicator.

## VII. Monitoring and Surveillance

The firm's transaction monitoring system shall generate alerts based on rule-based triggers and, where implemented, behavioral analytics. Alerts shall be reviewed within 48 hours of generation (24 hours for alerts classified as "high" severity). The determination of alert severity shall be made by the monitoring system based on configured thresholds, but may be manually upgraded by an analyst based on contextual factors discovered during review. An alert may be downgraded or dismissed only by an analyst with a minimum of 24 months of compliance experience, and the basis for dismissal must be documented in the case management system.

When reviewing alerts involving multiple accounts held by the same customer or by related parties (as identified by the firm's entity resolution process), the analyst shall aggregate activity across all associated accounts before making a determination. Failure to aggregate cross-account activity has been identified by FINRA as a recurring examination finding and is considered a supervisory deficiency.

## VIII. Escalation and Case Management

### A. Mandatory Escalation Triggers

The following situations require immediate escalation to a senior compliance officer or the CCO:

1. Any potential SAR filing (mandatory for junior analysts; see Section III(C)).
2. Any conflict between customer rights and investigation confidentiality (see Section V(B)).
3. Any transaction or pattern involving a Politically Exposed Person ("PEP").
4. Any situation in which the applicable policy provision is ambiguous or the analyst is uncertain as to the correct course of action.
5. Any customer communication that includes threats of litigation, regulatory complaint, or media exposure in connection with a compliance-related delay or denial.

### B. Case Documentation

All escalations, alert reviews, and transaction decisions shall be documented in the firm's case management system with a unique case identifier, the name and role of the assigned analyst, a chronological narrative of actions taken, and the final disposition. Case records shall be retained for a minimum of five years from the date of final disposition.

## IX. Amendments and Supplemental Guidance

This policy may be amended by the CCO with the approval of the firm's Board of Directors or its designated compliance committee. Supplemental guidance on topics not covered by this policy (including, without limitation, digital asset transactions, decentralized finance protocols, and non-fungible token transfers) will be issued as addenda to this policy as regulatory clarity develops. Until such supplemental guidance is issued, any transaction or activity involving a product or instrument type not explicitly addressed by this policy shall be escalated to the CCO for case-by-case review. Analysts shall not apply this policy by analogy to novel product types without prior written authorization from the CCO.

The firm acknowledges that regulatory requirements in the areas of AML, CTR, and SAR evolve over time and that this policy document may not reflect the most current guidance at all times. In the event of a conflict between this policy and applicable law or regulation, the law or regulation shall control.
