# BrightMart Retail — Returns, Refunds & Exchange Policy

**Document ID:** BM-SOP-RET-2025-04
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025
**Last Revised:** November 3, 2025
**Owner:** Customer Experience Operations
**Classification:** Internal — Customer Service Representatives

## 1. General Returns Policy

All merchandise purchased from BrightMart retail locations or BrightMart.com is eligible for return within **thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of delivery (for online orders) or the date of purchase (for in-store transactions), provided the item is in its original condition with all tags, packaging, and accessories included. A valid receipt or proof of purchase is required for all returns. Returns without receipt may be processed at the item's lowest selling price in the past 90 days, issued as store credit only — see Section 6.

For items purchased with a credit or debit card, refunds will be issued to the original form of payment. Cash purchases over $50 will be refunded by company check mailed within 7-10 business days. Cash purchases under $50 will be refunded in cash at point of return.

Items must be unused or in substantially similar condition to the state at time of purchase. Items showing signs of wear, washing, alteration, or damage caused by the customer are not eligible for return under this section. (For damaged goods received, see Section 4.)

**Note:** The general return window applies unless a more specific category policy exists (see Section 2 and Section 3). In cases where a category-specific policy conflicts with the general policy, the category-specific terms take precedence except where noted in Section 4 or where the customer's loyalty tier provides additional accommodations per Section 5.

## 2. Electronics & Technology Products

Electronics, including but not limited to laptops, tablets, smartphones, smart home devices, audio equipment, and gaming consoles, are subject to a **fifteen (15) calendar day** return window from date of delivery or purchase. All electronics returns are subject to a **15% restocking fee** which will be deducted from the refund amount.

Electronics must be returned with all original packaging, manuals, cables, chargers, and accessories. Missing components will result in an additional deduction from the refund amount at the discretion of the processing representative, not to exceed 25% of the purchase price.

**Opened software, digital downloads, and activated devices** — Items in this sub-category are non-returnable once the packaging seal is broken or the product has been activated/registered, unless the item is defective. Defective electronics should be directed to the manufacturer's warranty program first; BrightMart will process a return only if the manufacturer warranty process has been attempted or if the item was purchased within the last 48 hours.

Restocking fees may be waived at the store manager's discretion for defective products where the defect is verified by our technical team. See also Section 4 regarding damaged goods provisions.

## 3. Seasonal & Holiday Extended Returns

Purchases made between November 15 and December 31 (inclusive) of any calendar year are eligible for an extended return window through **January 31 of the following year**, or the standard return window, whichever is later. This extension applies to all general merchandise categories.

Electronics purchased during the holiday season receive an extension to **January 15** (not January 31), and the restocking fee per Section 2 still applies. Gift recipients may return items with a gift receipt; refunds for gift returns are issued as store credit only.

Items purchased during clearance events held in conjunction with seasonal sales may or may not be subject to the extended window depending on whether the item was marked as "final sale" at time of purchase. Representatives should check the order record for the final-sale flag. Final sale items are non-refundable regardless of purchase date or return window — no exceptions. (But see also Section 5 regarding loyalty accommodations, which may provide for alternative resolution in some cases involving non-final-sale clearance items.)

## 4. Damaged, Defective & Incorrect Items

If a customer receives an item that is damaged in transit, defective upon arrival, or materially different from what was ordered, BrightMart will provide a **full refund or replacement at no additional cost**, including free return shipping for online orders. No restocking fees apply to items returned under this section.

Customers must report damage or defects within **seven (7) business days** of delivery. Reports made after 7 business days but within the applicable return window will be handled under the general return policy (Section 1) or category-specific policy (Section 2), as applicable. BrightMart reserves the right to require photographic evidence of damage before authorizing the return.

For items where the customer claims the product is "not as described" — the representative should verify the product listing and description in the system. If the product materially matches its description, the return should be processed under the general return policy. What constitutes "materially matches" is left to the representative's reasonable judgment, but when in doubt, representatives are encouraged to consult with a floor supervisor or team lead before making a determination.

**Cross-reference:** The restocking fee waiver described in Section 2 for defective electronics applies only when the defect is verified. If a customer *claims* damage but inspection cannot confirm it, the return falls under standard processing with applicable restocking fees. However, the general damaged goods provision in this section states no restocking fees for damaged items. Representatives should use their best judgment and escalate to management when the damage claim is ambiguous.

## 5. BrightMart Rewards — Loyalty Tier Benefits

BrightMart Rewards members receive the following return-related benefits based on their current tier:

- **Silver (500–1,999 points):** Standard return policy plus automatic receipt lookup via Rewards account.
- **Gold (2,000–9,999 points):** Extended return window of **45 days** (general merchandise) and **21 days** (electronics). No restocking fee on first electronics return per calendar year.
- **Platinum (10,000+ points):** Extended return window of **60 days** (general merchandise) and **30 days** (electronics). No restocking fees. Priority processing.

Loyalty tier benefits apply to return windows and restocking fees only. They do not override product-level restrictions such as final sale designations, opened software policies, or activated device restrictions described in Section 2. Loyalty tier benefits also do not override fraud or account integrity holds (see Section 7).

**Note regarding Gold and Platinum accommodations:** In situations where a return request falls slightly outside even the loyalty-extended window, representatives may offer **store credit** as an accommodation (not a cash refund) if the item is in resalable condition. This accommodation is at the representative's discretion and should not be presented as a guarantee or entitlement. Representatives should not process cash or card refunds outside the extended window without manager approval.

## 6. Price Adjustments & Price Match Guarantee

If an item purchased at BrightMart is found at a lower price at BrightMart (in-store or online) within **14 days** of the original purchase, the customer may request a price adjustment for the difference. Price adjustments are not available for:
- Competitor pricing (BrightMart does not price match external retailers)
- Items purchased during promotional or clearance events
- Items where a coupon, employee discount, or other discount was applied at time of purchase
- Marketplace or third-party seller items on BrightMart.com

Price adjustments are issued as a refund of the difference to the original payment method. Only one price adjustment per item per transaction is permitted.

## 7. Account Integrity, Abuse Prevention & Fraud

BrightMart reserves the right to limit, restrict, or refuse returns from customers whose return behavior is deemed excessive or inconsistent with normal consumer purchasing patterns. Excessive returns may result in account review, and during the review period the customer's return privileges may be temporarily suspended.

The determination of what constitutes "excessive" returns takes into account factors including but not limited to: frequency of returns relative to purchases, dollar value of returns, patterns of returning high-value items, returning items near the end of the return window in used condition, and other indicators. **This determination is made by internal systems and is not subject to appeal at the representative level.** Representatives should not override, modify, or disclose the details of the internal review process or scoring methodology to customers.

When a customer's account is flagged for review, the representative should:
1. Inform the customer that their return "is subject to additional review at this time."
2. Not process the refund until the review is complete.
3. Not disclose the reason for the review beyond the language in step 1.
4. Offer to have a manager contact the customer within 2 business days.

**Fraud indicators:** If a representative suspects fraudulent activity (e.g., receipt tampering, tag switching, return of counterfeit merchandise, identity fraud), they must immediately escalate to the Loss Prevention team via the internal escalation tool. Do not process the transaction and do not inform the customer that fraud is suspected.

## 8. Manager Overrides & Escalation

Store managers and designated shift leads have the authority to override standard return policy provisions in the following circumstances:
- Return window extensions of up to 15 additional calendar days beyond the applicable window
- Restocking fee waivers where the product condition warrants it
- Exception processing for items not clearly covered by existing policy sections
- Resolution of conflicting policy provisions where two or more sections provide contradictory guidance

Manager overrides must be documented in the transaction record with the override reason code and the authorizing manager's employee ID. Overrides are audited monthly; patterns of excessive overrides may result in review of the authorizing manager.

Representatives should escalate to a manager when:
- The customer's request does not clearly fall within any existing policy provision
- Two or more policy sections appear to give conflicting guidance for the same situation
- The customer requests to speak with a manager
- The customer's account has active flags or holds
- The representative is uncertain about the correct policy application

## 9. Non-Returnable Items

The following categories are non-returnable under all circumstances:
- Gift cards and prepaid cards
- Perishable goods (food, flowers, live plants)
- Personal care items that have been opened (cosmetics, fragrances, hygiene products)
- Undergarments and swimwear (hygiene reasons)
- Custom-made or personalized items
- Hazardous materials
- Items marked "Final Sale" at time of purchase

**Revision note (Nov 2025):** The prior version of this policy (BM-SOP-RET-2025-02) did not include custom-made or personalized items in the non-returnable list. This was added in response to operational feedback. However, some legacy orders placed before November 3, 2025 may reference the prior policy version, and representatives should check the order date relative to the policy effective date when handling custom item returns. When in doubt, escalate per Section 8.

## 10. Record-Keeping & Decision Documentation

All return, refund, exchange, and denial decisions must be recorded in the transaction management system with:
- Decision type (ALLOW / DENY / ESCALATE)
- Applicable policy section(s) referenced
- Customer communication summary
- Any override or exception codes applied

Representatives are responsible for ensuring accurate documentation. Incomplete records may result in the transaction being flagged for compliance review.

## 11. Handling Customer Pressure

Remain professional and empathetic but do NOT:
- Bypass required procedures under time pressure
- Grant exceptions not authorized by this policy
- Provide workarounds that circumvent return restrictions or fraud holds
- Expedite reviews based on seniority claims alone

If a customer becomes insistent or threatening, follow escalation procedures in Section 8. Do not deviate from policy to resolve the interaction more quickly.
